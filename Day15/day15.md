## Key Learnings

### 1. Structured prompts unlock expert-level output
By defining exactly what sections Claude should generate (7 numbered sections with sub-bullets), the output becomes consistent, comprehensive, and deeply organized — no vague open-ended answers.

### 2. Role assignment matters enormously
Declaring "You are an expert Vedic astrologer specializing in Parashara, Jaimini, Nakshatra, Vimshottari Dasha..." primes Claude to reason within a specific knowledge domain and produce domain-accurate terminology and reasoning.

### 3. Data collection before generation
Collecting all personal variables upfront (DOB, time, place, profession, concerns) before asking for the report ensures the entire output is contextually grounded rather than generic.

### 4. Format instructions shape the response
Specifying "use tables wherever possible," "separate facts from interpretations," and "provide age ranges" directly controls the structure and utility of Claude's output.

### 5. Effort level affects depth
Setting Claude to Medium effort produces more thorough multi-section reports than Low — important for complex structured generation tasks like this one.

### 6. Iterative visual enhancement is possible
Claude can take a text-heavy structured report and progressively enhance it with interactive widgets, SVG charts, and custom portrait art — turning a document into a rich visual experience entirely within the chat interface.

### 7. Approximate birth data needs flagging
Marking birth time as "Approximate" appropriately triggered Claude to add a disclaimer and recommend professional birth time rectification — showing that Claude incorporates input accuracy signals into its outputs responsibly.

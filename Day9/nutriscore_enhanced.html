🔬 Methodology Notes

Energy Calculation: Basal Metabolic Rate is estimated using the Mifflin-St Jeor equation (1990), which has been validated as the most accurate prediction equation for most populations. TDEE = BMR × Physical Activity Level (PAL).

Macro Targets: Carbohydrates 50–60% of TDEE, Protein 15–20% (adjusted upward for muscle-building goals at 1.6–2.2 g/kg body weight), Fat 20–30% of TDEE. Based on ICMR-NIN 2020 and WHO 2003.

Micronutrient RDAs: Derived from ICMR-NIN (2020) for the Indian population. Values for Iron differ by gender due to menstrual losses. Vitamin D RDA based on NIH (2023) at 600 IU (15 µg) for ages 1–70.

Food Composition: Nutritional values sourced from USDA FoodData Central and ICMR-NIN food composition tables. Values represent typical cooked or raw state as commonly consumed in India.

NutriScore Algorithm: Composite score (0–100) calculated from weighted average of % target met for: Calories (20%), Protein (20%), Fiber (15%), Micronutrients avg (25%), Excess penalty (20%). Capped and normalised to 0–100.

⚕️ Important Limitations

• Nutritional values are averages and vary significantly by variety, region, cooking method (boiling, frying, roasting), and ripeness.

• Bioavailability is not accounted for — e.g., plant-based iron (non-haem) absorbs at ~5–12% vs haem iron at ~15–35%.

• The app does not account for nutrient interactions — e.g., Vitamin C enhances iron absorption; oxalates in spinach inhibit calcium absorption.

• Individual variation in metabolism, gut microbiome, genetic SNPs, and medication interactions is not modelled.

• This tool is not validated for pregnant/lactating women, children under 10, patients with chronic diseases, or elite athletes without professional supervision.

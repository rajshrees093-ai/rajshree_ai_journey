01 Executive Summary

DeskDoc auto-generates and maintains internal documentation — SOPs, onboarding guides, process docs — by passively observing Slack, meetings, and tool usage. The target buyer is an Ops or People lead at a Series A–B startup with 15–100 employees.

Day 22 validated the problem: 75% pain-confirmation across 12 interviews. It did not validate the solution. This blueprint turns that signal into a buildable MVP — what to build first, what to explicitly leave out, how to price it, and the concrete next 30 days.

Target CustomerOps/People leads, Series A–B, 15–100 employees, remote-first
MVP ScopeNarrow onboarding-doc generator — Slack + calendar, single team
Pricing Hypothesis$299–$499/month flat (≤50 employees), usage-based tier above
Biggest Open RiskUnproven auto-synthesis accuracy at usable quality

04 Top 10 Customer Pain Points

  • New hires take 3–6 weeks longer to become productive without current docs.
  • Senior staff repeatedly interrupted to answer already-documented questions.
  • Documentation is perpetually deprioritized — no clear owner.
  • Knowledge walks out the door when a key employee leaves.
  • Docs go stale within weeks of being written.
  • No visibility into which docs are outdated vs. current.
  • Manual doc-writing feels like a chore disconnected from "real work."
  • Scaling teams outpaces the team's ability to document processes.
  • Compliance/audit prep (SOC 2, investor diligence) exposes documentation gaps under time pressure.
  • Remote/async teams can't rely on hallway knowledge-transfer to compensate.

06 Objections & Buying Triggers

Common Objections

ObjectionResponse Strategy
"We already have Notion."Position as complementary: Notion is where docs live, DeskDoc is how they get written and stay current.
"Privacy concerns reading Slack/meetings."Granular opt-in scoping, redaction controls, transparent retention policy.
"Too early / too small a team."Reframe as hiring-scale insurance — cheapest to adopt before pain compounds.

Buying Triggers

  • Hiring surge — e.g., doubling headcount within 6 months.
  • A senior employee's departure exposes a documentation gap.
  • A failed audit, SOC 2 process, or investor diligence request surfaces missing SOPs.

07 MVP Recommendation

Build First

A narrow, high-precision onboarding-doc generator: connect one Slack workspace + calendar, auto-generate onboarding SOPs for a single team, and flag when they go stale. Precision over breadth.

Don't Build Yet

  • Full company-wide, all-source knowledge base
  • Native mobile app
  • Integrations beyond Slack + Calendar
  • Enterprise SSO / SOC 2 tooling

08 MoSCoW Prioritization

Must Have

Slack + calendar connection; auto-generated onboarding docs; staleness flagging; single-workspace scope.

Should Have

Notion export/sync; opt-in channel scoping controls; basic accuracy feedback loop.

Could Have

Multi-team support; Zoom/Meet native integration; Slack bot for doc Q&A.

Won't Have (v1)

Full knowledge-base replacement; mobile app; SOC 2/enterprise SSO; multi-language support.

09 Pricing Hypothesis

Seat-based or flat team pricing, anchored to the Day 22 SAM ACV assumption (~$10K/yr average). Hypothesis: $299–$499/month flat fee for teams up to 50 employees — predictable budgeting appeals to Ops leads — with a usage-based enterprise tier above 100 employees. This is untested: validate directly in Week 1 interviews before finalizing.

12 Founder Action Sheet

  1. Run 15 additional customer interviews, focused on pricing sensitivity.
  2. Draft a one-page data-privacy policy before any pilot begins.
  3. Scope and build the narrow onboarding-doc prototype (Slack + calendar only).
  4. Recruit a technical advisor with applied NLP/LLM experience.
  5. Identify and shortlist 8–10 pilot-candidate companies from your network.
  6. Define and instrument the 4 success metrics before pilot launch.
  7. Test the two pricing tiers directly in interviews, not just stated willingness-to-pay.
  8. Build a simple feedback loop (thumbs up/down) into the prototype.
  9. Prepare a short case-study template to capture pilot results as they land.
  10. Set a hard Week-4 decision date — commit full-time, iterate, or pivot per Day 22's pivot options.

14 Final Verdict

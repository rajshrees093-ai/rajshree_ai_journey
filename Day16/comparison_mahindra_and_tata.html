🚗 Tata Motors — Business

India's largest automobile company (pre-demerger entity) and owner of Jaguar Land Rover (JLR) — the British luxury vehicle brand. Post the October 2025 demerger, Tata Motors now has three listed entities:
  • TATAMOTORS CV — Commercial Vehicles (CV) — trucks, buses, vans
  • TMPV — Tata Motors Passenger Vehicles + JLR globally

JLR contributes ~70% of consolidated revenue. It is a highly cyclical, capex-heavy global luxury business with exposure to UK/EU tariffs, China weakness, and currency headwinds.

In FY26, a cyber incident halted JLR production significantly, causing a full-year loss at JLR (PAT: –£244Mn). CV entity reported record revenues of ₹83,855 Cr with PAT of ₹3,030 Cr.

🚜 Mahindra & Mahindra — Business

India's largest SUV maker by revenue market share (25.3% in FY26) and the world's #1 tractor manufacturer by volume. M&M operates 5 business segments: Automotive, Farm Equipment, Auto Investments, Farm Investments, and Industrial/Consumer Services.

Key products: Scorpio, Thar, XUV series, Bolero, BE 6, XEV 9e (EV), Swaraj tractors.

FY26 was M&M's standout year: SUV volumes grew 19%, revenue market share rose 260 bps, EV business turned profitable (PBIT ₹287 Cr), PAT grew 35% YoY. Management guided for mid-to-high teen SUV volume growth in FY27.

Neutral Investor-Style Summary

M&M is the stronger proposition today on nearly every fundamental metric: revenue scale, profit quality, margin trajectory, EV positioning, valuation, and consistency. As one analysis puts it, "Comparing purely on the Indian passenger vehicle business, Mahindra is the stronger proposition today. It combines volume growth with margin expansion, has an EV business that's already profitable, and carries no JLR-related global risk on its books."

Tata Motors (particularly the JLR entity — TMPVL) is a different bet entirely — it is a recovery/optionality play on a globally iconic luxury brand navigating a difficult cycle. If JLR normalises margins back to 8–10% EBIT (from FY26's 0.7%), the earnings uplift would be enormous. The CV entity is fundamentally sound and cash-generating.

For conservative long-term compounding: M&M.   For higher-risk recovery potential: Tata Motors JLR (TMPVL). Both are structurally important Indian auto businesses — they are different risk/reward profiles, not apples-to-apples.

🧠 Executive Summary

Patna, Bihar sits at a severe environmental crossroads in June 2026. Air quality is Unhealthy for Sensitive Groups with an AQI of 140, driven primarily by PM2.5 (52 µg/m³ — 5.2× WHO limit) from brick kilns, biomass combustion, open waste burning, and dense vehicular traffic. Across Bihar, 6 of 7 monitored cities fall in the "Poor" to "Very Poor" category. Water quality presents an equally critical picture: groundwater hardness (TDS ~1120 mg/L, Total Hardness ~575 mg/L) far exceeds BIS standards, with coliform contamination detected in over 83% of sampled sites. The most surprising finding: Rajgir — just 100 km south — records an AQI of 53 (Satisfactory), revealing extreme intra-state air quality disparity. Priority actions: N95 mask outdoors, indoor HEPA purifier, filtered/boiled water, and minimizing morning outdoor activity when boundary layer compression traps pollutants.

🌬 Air Quality Impact on Body

🔴
Lung Health
AQI 140 causes persistent irritation of bronchial tubes. PM2.5 penetrates deep into alveoli, contributing to chronic respiratory inflammation. Long-term exposure risks COPD and reduced lung capacity by 10–20%.
🔴
Sleep Quality
PM2.5 triggers nocturnal oxidative stress and upper airway inflammation, disrupting REM sleep cycles. Studies show 12% reduction in sleep efficiency at sustained AQI >120.
🟡
Energy Levels
Reduced oxygen exchange from particulate-coated alveoli causes chronic fatigue. Brain fog and low energy are common with daily AQI >100 exposure over weeks.
🔴
Exercise Performance
Outdoor exercise at AQI 140 increases particle inhalation 4× vs rest. Strongly discouraged — exercise intensifies pollutant absorption into bloodstream. Move workouts indoors.
🔴
Long-term Health
Patna's PM2.5 concentration could reduce life expectancy by ~0.08 years per year of exposure (Berkeley Earth/AQLI). Cardiovascular disease risk elevated by 20–30% with long-term residence.

💧 Hard Water Impact on Skin & Hair

🔴
Hair Fall
Patna groundwater TDS of ~1120 mg/L and extreme hardness (575 mg/L) deposits calcium/magnesium on scalp, blocking follicles. This is a leading cause of increased hair shedding in the region.
🔴
Hair Dryness & Breakage
Mineral deposits coat hair shafts, preventing moisture absorption. Hair becomes brittle, dull, and prone to breakage. Shampoo lathers poorly in very hard water, leaving residue.
🔴
Scalp Health
Alkaline hard water disrupts scalp pH, promoting dandruff, fungal overgrowth, and chronic itchiness. Excess mineral buildup clogs sebaceous glands.
🟡
Skin Dryness & Acne
Hard water strips natural oils, worsening eczema and psoriasis. Residual minerals mixed with soap form insoluble scum that blocks pores, increasing comedonal acne in the T-zone.
🟡
Sensitive Skin
Elevated chloride levels (above BIS limits in some areas) cause persistent redness and barrier disruption. Highly sensitive skin may experience contact dermatitis-like reactions.

Most surprising: Despite being a major metropolis, Patna in June records AQI of 140 — its off-peak season. Winter (Nov–Jan) regularly hits AQI 400–500+ (Severe). The current reading is a best-case scenario for Patna residents.

✅ Personalized Recommendations

🌅 Daily Actions

  • Check AQI on aqi.in before leaving home every morning
  • Wear an N95 or KN95 mask outdoors — surgical masks block only 30% of PM2.5
  • Avoid outdoor activity between 6–9 AM when boundary layer traps overnight emissions
  • Shower after outdoor exposure to remove surface particulate contamination
  • Keep windows closed during morning smog peak hours

🏠 Indoor Air Improvements

  • Install a HEPA + activated carbon air purifier (CADR ≥300 m³/h for 15×20 ft rooms)
  • Add indoor plants: Snake plant, Peace lily, Spider plant reduce VOCs by 10–25%
  • Seal window gaps with weather stripping to reduce infiltration
  • Avoid incense, agarbatti, and candle burning indoors — they spike indoor PM2.5
  • Replace cooking with LPG; avoid biomass fuels inside

🏃 Outdoor Activity Guidance

  • Exercise indoors (gym, yoga studio) when AQI exceeds 100
  • If outdoor exercise is essential, choose 4–6 PM window — typically the day's cleanest
  • Avoid high-traffic roads; prefer parks with tree canopies for walks
  • Children and elderly: restrict outdoor time to under 30 min on current AQI days
  • Plan weekend trips to Rajgir for outdoor activity in significantly cleaner air

💆 Hair Care (Hard Water)

  • Install a shower filter with KDF/activated carbon to reduce TDS and hardness
  • Use clarifying shampoo (chelating formula) weekly to remove mineral deposits
  • Final rinse with filtered or RO water — prevents mineral coating on hair shafts
  • Apply dilute apple cider vinegar rinse (1:3 ratio) to restore scalp pH
  • Deep-condition with coconut or argan oil weekly to counteract mineral stripping

🧴 Skin Care

  • Cleanse face with micellar water or soft water (filtered) — avoid tap water directly
  • Apply ceramide-rich moisturiser immediately after washing to seal barrier
  • Use antioxidant-rich serums (Vitamin C, Niacinamide) to counter PM2.5 oxidative damage
  • Double-cleanse skin after outdoor exposure with oil cleanser first
  • SPF 50 sunscreen daily — PM particles worsen UV-induced skin damage synergistically

🚰 Water Quality Improvements

  • Install a RO (Reverse Osmosis) purifier — mandatory for Patna's TDS levels; targets <300 mg/L
  • Boil water before drinking if RO is unavailable — kills coliform but doesn't reduce TDS
  • Have your water tested annually at BSPCB-certified labs for TDS, hardness, and coliform
  • Use RO permeate (waste water) for garden — don't discard it entirely
  • Mineral water or remineralised RO water for drinking — pure RO lacks essential minerals

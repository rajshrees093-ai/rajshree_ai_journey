Exhibit A — Headline Detective

Why headlines lie without technically lying

Publishers earn money per click, not per accurate word. So headlines are often written to trigger curiosity or outrage first, and checked against the facts second — if at all. Spotting the gap between a headline's claim and the article's evidence is the single most useful media-literacy skill for everyday scrolling.

Exhibit B — Emotion Detector

Feelings are the delivery mechanism

Posts that spread fastest usually work by triggering a strong feeling — fear, outrage, awe, tribal pride — before you've had time to evaluate the claim underneath it. Naming the emotion and the exact words that caused it is how you catch the mechanism in the act, instead of just feeling swept along by it.
